01 — About

Hi, I'm Andrew.

I'm a software engineer at WellHive, where I work on Vigil, our internal governance, risk, and compliance platform. Before that I spent nearly five years at Green XT building Weblog — an enterprise system that replaced six legacy applications and serves thousands of U.S. Navy personnel.

I came up in the Microsoft ecosystem and that's where I got good. Five years of C# and ASP.NET Core, Entity Framework Core and SQL Server, Razor Pages and Telerik UI — building data-heavy interfaces for people who use them all day, every day. Leading a six-application consolidation in that stack taught me more about software than any course did: how to model a domain that has absorbed a decade of edge cases, how to migrate users who have every reason to distrust another rewrite, and how much of engineering is really about earning trust.

These days I work in TypeScript and Go — React on the front, Express and Postgres behind it, infrastructure in Pulumi shipped to AWS GovCloud. Different tools, same instinct: in regulated environments "it works" isn't the finish line. The system also has to be auditable, access-controlled, and defensible to an assessor months after you've moved on.

Outside of work I've been married a little over three years and recently became a father. Most of my free time goes to a disc golf course, a Stephen King novel, or the next record on a very long list.
