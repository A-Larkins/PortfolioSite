I built a small C# console application to support the 52 Week Movie Challenge, a movie logbook I found and started because I love movies. The book is organized into themed categories, and each week you pick and watch a film that fits a specific prompt, then log detailed information about it. I realized I was spending way too much time clicking around IMDb and Google to gather the same data for every movie, so I wrote a helper app that pulls everything together for me. The app queries multiple movie APIs (OMDb and TMDB), combines the results, and outputs all the key details I need in one place. For information that isn’t easily available via APIs—like aspect ratio or certain awards details—the app automatically opens targeted browser searches and IMDb quote pages so I can grab the remaining info quickly. I published it as a self-contained executable and added it to my system path, so I can just type a single command in the terminal, enter a movie title, and log a film in seconds instead of bouncing between websites.
