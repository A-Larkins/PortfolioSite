At WellHive I work on an internal governance, risk, and compliance platform — the system the company uses to manage security controls, evidence, and continuous monitoring for a FedRAMP High environment.

I joined the team recently, so the platform was well established before I arrived. My work is feature development on an existing codebase: picking up requirements, building them out across the stack, and getting them through review and into the product.

The stack is TypeScript end to end. React on the front, Express services behind it, Drizzle ORM over PostgreSQL, all deployed to AWS. Coming from years of C# and Entity Framework, a lot of it mapped over more directly than I expected — an ORM is an ORM, and the questions you ask about a schema don't change with the language.

The compliance domain is the genuinely new part. Working somewhere that has to satisfy FedRAMP High means the requirements come with a paper trail attached: what changed, who approved it, and whether you can demonstrate that months later to someone who wasn't there. It's a useful constraint to design under, and it's made me more careful about the things that are easy to skip when nobody is going to audit them.
