I'm working through <em>1001 Albums You Must Hear Before You Die</em> and tracking it in Google Sheets — the master list with my ratings, a starred "must hear" list, and my own replacement picks. Keeping those three in sync by hand meant copying rows between tabs and renumbering everything by hand. So I built an app to do it.

C# on .NET 10, Avalonia for the UI, packaged as a macOS app. It reads and writes the Google Sheets API in both directions, so the spreadsheet stays the source of truth and I can still edit it from my phone.

What it does:

• Sync is a reconcile pass, not an overwrite — it works out what's actually different, adds starred albums missing from the must-hear list, drops rows that shouldn't be there, and places new entries by year before renumbering.<br>
• A read-only check tells me what a sync would change before anything gets written.<br>
• Adding a replacement validates against both lists first, so no duplicates.<br>
• Rating session for working through albums one at a time.<br>
• Searchable viewer, PDF export via QuestPDF, and it scrapes Discogs to refresh the canonical list.

Built with AI assistance, which is most of why it exists — the idea had been sitting on my list for a long time.
