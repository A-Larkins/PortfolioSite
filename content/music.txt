I’ve always been passionate about music. It can capture so much that’s hard to describe, and my favorite thing to do is go to a concert. I’m into rock and metal, but I listen to all kinds of music.

I started playing drums around age 10 and took lessons for a while, but I didn’t like the structure—I just wanted to play. In high school, my friends and I would jam, and even though I never got super serious about drums, they’re still fun. Eventually I shifted my focus to guitar.

When I was about 16, I bought a cheap acoustic guitar and didn’t even tune it at first. Later, when I started learning programming, I struggled with it. A friend suggested I learn the Rubik’s Cube first, saying the algorithms would help me understand logical steps. Once I learned to solve it, I realized I could apply the same approach to guitar—and programming.

Ever since then, I’ve approached both guitar and coding the same way: it’s rewarding to work through something that feels impossible at first. If you break it down, stay consistent, and keep pushing, you eventually get there.
