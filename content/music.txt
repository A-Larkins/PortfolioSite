I’ve always been passionate about music. It captures things that are hard to put into words, and honestly my favorite thing in the world is going to concerts. I’m mostly into rock and metal, but I listen to a little bit of everything.

I started playing drums around age 10 and took lessons for a while, but I never loved the structure—I just wanted to play. In high school my friends and I would jam for fun, and while I never got super serious about drums, I still enjoy them. Eventually, I shifted my focus to guitar.

When I was about 16, I bought a cheap acoustic guitar and didn’t even bother tuning it at first. Around that same time, I started learning programming and struggled with it. A friend suggested I learn how to solve a Rubik’s Cube first, saying the algorithms would help me understand logical steps. Once I figured it out, something clicked—I realized the same approach worked for guitar, and for coding too.

Since then, I’ve treated both the same way: things often feel impossible at first, but if you break them down, stay consistent, and keep pushing, they eventually start to make sense. That process is what I find rewarding.
